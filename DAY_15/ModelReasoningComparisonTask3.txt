Critical Clinical Issues
1. Inadequate rule-out of acute coronary syndrome (ACS)

Chest pain x 2 days is a classic ACS presentation
Stable vitals + unremarkable exam do not exclude ACS — many MIs present this way
EKG alone is insufficient — troponin is the standard of care for ruling out myocardial infarction
Discharging without troponin is below the standard of care and exposes the patient to real harm

2. Inappropriate discharge medication

Ibuprofen for undifferentiated chest pain is not evidence-based
No indication documented; pain duration and character not recorded
No documented risk stratification (HEART score, TIMI, etc.)

3. Vague follow-up plan

"Follow up if symptoms worsen" is inadequate
No clear safety-net instructions, no timeframe, no alternative contact method
